The Lego Test

Obstacle avoidance, ranked

With young kids and small toys on the floor, this is the feature that matters most day to day.

AIVI 3D
★★★★★

Camera + structured light. Identifies objects down to ~5cm. Recognises toys, cords, shoes, pet waste. Photos in-app.

Still misses very small items (cat-toy size).
Camera AI
★★★★

Camera-based detection. Excellent cord avoidance, solid toy avoidance. Less refined for very small items.

Camera-only — struggles in low light.
Reactive AI
★★★★★

Camera + structured light + cross-projection. Lifting chassis climbs over obstacles. Industry-leading edge cases.

Cannot reliably ID items under ~5cm.
OmniDirt
★★★★★

Structured light + RGB camera. Detects dirt type and adjusts cleaning. Strong small-object recognition.

Newer platform — fewer long-term reviews yet.
Honest advice: No robot vacuum can reliably dodge every Lego brick. A quick floor tidy before a scheduled run is still the best defence. These four are the best available at trying.
Decision Guide

Which one is yours?

Ecovacs X8 Pro Omni — Best All-Rounder

  • Strongest all-rounder as rated by PCMag Australia
  • Self-cleaning roller mop mid-run — no spreading dirty water
  • Best value at 50% off RRP
  • Mix of carpet + hard floors (highest 15mm mop lift)
  • Strong AI obstacle avoidance

Eufy S1 Pro — Best for Mopping

  • Mopping is your #1 priority — HydroJet is best-in-class
  • Lowest price at $999
  • Brand familiarity — Amy's dad owns Eufy
  • Mostly hard floors with some carpet
  • Eufy app ecosystem and experience

Roborock Qrevo Curv — Best Navigator

  • Thresholds or multi-level — lifting chassis is unique
  • Edge and corner cleaning — extendable arm is best tested
  • Highest suction (18,500 Pa)
  • Premium features justify the $250 over the X8
  • Curved aesthetic and Roborock build quality

Dreame X40 Ultra — Premium Edge Cleaning

  • MopExtend™ arm reaches where others can't
  • Strong obstacle AI with OmniDirt detection
  • Slimmest profile at 96mm — fits more furniture
  • Canstar Innovation Award winner
  • Budget stretches to ~$2,000
